Swami Vivekananda - A Visionary Leader

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was a renowned Indian monk and spiritual leader who introduced the philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He is best remembered for his inspiring speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893, where he represented Hinduism and spoke of universal acceptance, tolerance, and harmony among different religions. His teachings emphasized self-realization, devotion, and service to humanity.

Swami Vivekananda's contributions to India and the world remain an eternal source of inspiration. His vision for a modern, strong India was founded on the principles of education, unity, and spiritual awakening. He is also known for his deep knowledge of the ancient texts of Hinduism, his advocacy for women’s empowerment, and his call for youth to dedicate themselves to the nation’s service.
